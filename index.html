Smart post-processing

Optional LLM pass cleans up filler words, adds punctuation, and formats lists. Toggle it per-session.

History, always

Works anywhere

Any app that accepts text: browsers, editors, chat, Figma, the terminal. It pastes into the focused field.

Notarized & auto-updating

Bring your own key

Your keys. Your provider. Your bill.

Voice never touches a server we operate. There's no sign-up, no subscription, no telemetry, no "free tier" meant to trap you. Paste your own API key in Settings and the audio goes directly from your Mac to the provider you chose.

No vendor lock-in

Switch providers in Settings and restart. Nothing migrates, because nothing was ever stored with us.

No accounts

There's no backend. You download the app, point it at a provider, and it works. That's the whole onboarding.

No data retention

Audio is sent to your chosen provider and discarded. Transcripts live locally on your disk, where you can inspect them.

Open options

Groq, Sarvam, OpenAI — pick whichever fits your latency, language, and budget. Add more as they're released.

🇮🇳 Made for India

Hindi, Tamil, Marathi, Telugu, Kannada, Bengali, Gujarati — and your accent.

Most dictation tools are trained on American English. When you speak Hinglish in a Bangalore café or dictate an email in Tamil, they choke. Voice ships with first-class support for Sarvam AI — built in India, trained on Indian speech — so you can dictate in the language you actually think in.

  • Saarika v2.5 & Saaras v3 — Sarvam's best STT models, selectable in Settings.
  • 12+ Indian languages including Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Marathi, Gujarati, Kannada, Malayalam, Punjabi, Odia.
  • Code-switching — natural Hinglish, Tanglish, and Kanglish without you toggling anything.
  • Indian accents in English work too. No more the-app-thinks-you-said-"vibe" when you said "five".

Privacy that's not a marketing slide

What stays on your Mac

  • Transcripts (last 500, in ~/Library/Application Support/voice/)
  • Your API keys (in the same local store, no keychain roundtrip by default)
  • Settings (language, hotkey, provider choices)

What leaves your Mac

  • Audio — sent to the STT provider you configured. That's it.
  • (Optional) Transcript — sent to an LLM for cleanup, if you enable post-processing.
  • Nothing else. No analytics, no crash reports, no "anonymous usage" payload.
